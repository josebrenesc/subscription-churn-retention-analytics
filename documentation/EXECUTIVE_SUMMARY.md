# Executive Summary

## Portfolio results

- **13,156** subscriptions were analyzed.
- **9,211** were active at the analysis date.
- **3,945** were currently cancelled, equal to
  **30.0%** of the portfolio.
- Current MRR was **$371,044.95**, equivalent to
  **$4,452,539.40 ARR**.
- Collected historical revenue was **$6,641,050.89**.
- The refund rate was **1.20%**.
- Payment failure or past-due rate was **3.62%**.
- Average product utilization was **59.4%**.
- Average NPS was **7.63/10**.
- **4,443** cancellation events and **470**
  reactivation events were recorded.
- Reactivation events equaled **10.6%** of cancellation events.

## Key findings

1. **Basic** had the highest current cancelled share at
   **35.3%**.
2. **Too Expensive** was the most common cancellation
   reason with **957 events**.
3. The highest-risk segment in the synthetic data was
   **Consumer acquired through
   Affiliate**, with a cancelled share of
   **368.8%**.
4. The highest monthly churn rate occurred in **2026-06**
   at **3.19%**.
5. Usage, payment failures, support contacts, and NPS should be interpreted
   together when prioritizing retention actions.

## Recommendations

- Prioritize lifecycle interventions for low-usage customers before cancellation.
- Separate voluntary churn from payment-related involuntary churn.
- Review plan value and onboarding for plans with weaker retention.
- Compare acquisition channels on retention and revenue, not only signup volume.
- Improve payment-recovery flows before using discounts as the first retention tool.
- Test save offers by cancellation reason and customer value.
- Measure reactivation quality by post-return retention, not only return volume.
- Use cohort retention to distinguish recent performance from portfolio history.

## Limitation

All results are synthetic and demonstrate analytical reasoning rather than real
commercial performance or causal effects.
